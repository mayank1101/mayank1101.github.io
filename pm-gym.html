Systems Thinking

Vision & Strategy

Vision-to-roadmap Strategy kernel Trade-offs

Business Outcomes & Product Outcomes

Category 03

Problem Framing, Validation, Metrics & Growth

Eleven frameworks for the middle and back half of the product cycle: pin down the real problem, generate and validate ideas, measure whether any of it worked, then position and grow it. Work through them in order, or jump straight to any topic.

1

Defining The Problem

Solve the right problem before you solve a problem right — writing a sharp problem statement and digging to the root cause with the 5 Whys.

Problem statement 5 Whys Root cause
2

Hypothesis Testing

Turn a guess into something you can prove wrong — writing a falsifiable hypothesis, finding your riskiest assumption, and designing a test before you build.

Falsifiable Leap of faith Success criteria
3

Ideation in Design Thinking

Generate wildly, then narrow deliberately — the ground rules for brainstorming, a toolkit of techniques, and how to converge without killing good ideas.

Divergent thinking Crazy 8s Dot voting
4

Product Ideation Framework

A repeatable path from insight to a shortlist worth building — writing How Might We questions and evaluating ideas on desirability, feasibility, and viability.

How Might We Ideation funnel Feasibility
5

Mind Maps

Think in branches, not bullet points — building a radial mind map and knowing when a different tool, like a KPI tree or affinity map, fits better.

Radial structure Central node Affinity map
6

Metrics

Pick the number that actually tells you the truth — the 3 A's of a good metric, leading vs. lagging indicators, and choosing a North Star.

North Star Leading indicator Vanity metric
7

A/B Testing

Let real users, not opinions, settle the debate — designing a valid test, statistical significance basics, and the pitfalls that quietly wreck a result.

Control group Significance Peeking
8

Analysis

Turn data into a decision, not just a chart — quantitative vs. qualitative methods, cohort and funnel analysis, and the biases that quietly distort a conclusion.

Cohort analysis Correlation vs. causation Simpson's Paradox
9

Decoding Growth

Real growth is what stays, not just what you add — the leaky bucket, the AARRR funnel, why retention is the engine, and earned vs. bought growth.

Leaky bucket AARRR Retention
10

Product Positioning

Set the context so customers instantly get your value — April Dunford's building blocks, the positioning statement, and the traps that make good products look confusing.

Competitive alternatives Frame of reference Differentiation
11

Understanding Growth Loops

Turn output back into input so growth compounds — loops vs. funnels, the anatomy of a loop, the three loop types, and why loops compound or stall.

Loops vs. funnels Viral / content / paid Loop factor
