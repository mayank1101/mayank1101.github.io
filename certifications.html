Why I Keep Learning

The technology landscape moves fast, and what's cutting-edge today is a baseline tomorrow. For a product manager, that means two things: understanding the technical possibilities and constraints well enough to make smart product decisions, and building product intuition that keeps user needs at the center regardless of how the technology shifts.

These certifications represent deliberate learning across both dimensions:

  • Technical Credibility: Understanding the systems I'm designing and evaluating — not just surface-level
  • Product Craft: Frameworks for roadmapping, prioritization, experimentation, and measuring real impact
  • Evaluation Rigor: Building the right metrics and feedback loops, not just shipping features
  • Analytics Discipline: Learning how to use data to drive product decisions and prove value
  • Validated Expertise: Demonstrating knowledge that complements real-world product delivery

Certifications validate structured knowledge — but real expertise shows up in shipped products. See how I apply these skills in my Token By Token, or read about the products I've owned and the outcomes they created on my about page.
