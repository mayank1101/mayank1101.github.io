About Me

I'm a Data Scientist making a deliberate move into Product Management. For 4.5+ years, I've built Analytics & AI systems, including Regulatory Intelligence & Analytics platforms, NLP pipelines, RAG-powered products, healthcare AI, and found that the most meaningful part of that work was always figuring out what to build and why. At Lamipak, I owned products end-to-end, talking to customers, defining roadmaps, aligning stakeholders, and coordinating with engineering teams to deliver. My data science background shapes how I approach decisions: with care about evidence, honest trade-offs, and a focus on what users actually need.

M.Tech CSE - Data Science, IIT Jammu · India

My Story: The Transition to Product

My M.Tech in Data Science at IIT Jammu taught me to think rigorously about evidence — what the data actually shows, not what we wish it showed. That discipline shaped everything that followed.

At nference.ai, I built production AI systems for healthcare: NLP pipelines using BioClinicalBERT and SciBERT, knowledge distillation that reduced model size by 8× while preserving performance, and multimodal AI combining medical imaging and clinical text. The key insight: technical impressiveness means nothing if users don't want it. That's when I started thinking more carefully about product decisions.

At Lamipak, I owned LamiOps and LamiTracker as product owner. LamiOps served 10K+ daily users. LamiTracker spanned 200+ sources across 18+ countries. I talked to customers, identified problems, defined roadmaps, aligned stakeholders, and coordinated with AI, backend, and frontend teams to deliver. That experience made something clear: figuring out what to build and why is the work I find most meaningful.

Today, I'm completing a Product Management Fellowship at NextLeap to make the transition deliberate. I write Token by Token to share how I think about AI and the products built on top of it.

Impact: Why This Transition Makes Sense

Evidence-Based Decision Making

I try to design metrics that reveal what's actually happening, not just what looks good in a report. Published research (AAAI 2024, NEJM AI). Partnership with Data & Engineering Teams

Having worked in data science and coordinated closely with AI engineers and backend developers, I can have grounded conversations about what's feasible, what's costly, and what trade-offs matter. These cross-functional partnerships are among the most important parts of product work, and my technical background helps me contribute meaningfully to them.

User-Centric Problem Solving

The most consistent thing I've learned is the best products start with understanding real user problems, not with impressive technology. At nference, working with healthcare professionals shaped how I thought about what a product needs to do. At Lamipak, owning products used by thousands of users taught me to think carefully about what to prioritize and what to leave out.
