My Story: The Transition to Product

My journey began with a fundamental belief: the best solutions are built on understanding users and data. During my M.Tech in Data Science at IIT Jammu, I learned to think analytically about problems — but I quickly realized that technology alone doesn't drive impact. It's how you apply it to solve real user problems that matters.

At nference.ai, I built production NLP systems clinicians actually trust. I optimized models for real constraints (9× speedup, 8× compression), championed evaluation rigor, and learned that technical excellence is worthless unless it solves user problems. This is where I understood the difference between engineering for metrics and engineering for impact.

At Lamipak, I made the deliberate shift toward product thinking. I owned LamiOps (10K+ daily users) and LamiTracker (200+ sources across 18+ countries). I defined product strategy, aligned stakeholders, measured success with rigorous KPIs, and realized: product leadership is about translating ambiguous problems into solutions users love and businesses need. This is what excited me. Not optimizing models. Not building systems. Building products that matter.

Today, I'm intentionally transitioning into Product Management. My AI and analytics background isn't baggage — it's my competitive advantage. I understand engineering trade-offs, can credibly partner with technical teams, and make better product decisions grounded in data. This rare combination of technical depth and user-focused product thinking is what I bring to PM roles. I write Token by Token — technical deep-dives on building products at scale — to share this perspective.
