My Story: The Transition to Product

My journey began with a fundamental belief: the best solutions are built on understanding users and data. During my M.Tech in Data Science at IIT Jammu, I learned to think analytically about problems — but I quickly realized that technology alone doesn't drive impact. It's how you apply it to solve real user problems that matters.

At nference.ai, I worked on products that healthcare professionals actually used and trusted. I learned that building something technically impressive means nothing if users don't want it. This is where I started thinking like a product person— defining what matters, measuring the right things, and ensuring every optimization served user needs.

At Lamipak, I owned products end-to-end. LamiOps served 10K+ daily users. LamiTracker grew to span 200+ sources across 18+ countries. I defined strategy, aligned teams, and built products that solved real business problems. I realized: product leadership is about understanding what users need, translating that into strategy, and executing relentlessly. This is what excited me. Not optimization. Not building systems. Building products that matter.

Today, I'm intentionally transitioning into Product Management. My data and analytics background is my competitive advantage— I can design metrics that reveal truth, make decisions grounded in evidence, and speak credibly with technical teams about trade-offs. But more importantly, I've spent years thinking about users and what they need. I write Token by Token — deep thinking on building products that matter — to share this perspective.
