PM Gym

Understanding Growth Loops

Turn output back into input so growth compounds

A funnel is linear — pour users in the top, some fall out the bottom, and you must keep refilling forever. A growth loop feeds the output of one cycle back in as the input to the next, so the product helps grow itself. This guide covers how loops work, their common types, and why they compound where funnels only leak.

Step-by-step lessons

Think in Loops, Not Just Funnels

Four short lessons: loops vs. funnels, the anatomy of a loop, the three common types, and why loops compound or stall.

1

Loops vs. Funnels

The classic model of growth is a funnel: a one-way path where you pour users in the top and a fraction convert at the bottom. It's linear — to keep growing, you must keep pouring more in. A growth loop is circular: the output of each cycle becomes the input to the next, so growth can feed itself.

Linear

Funnel

One-way. Needs constant external refilling at the top. Asks: "how do we get more in?"

Circular

Growth loop

Output reinvested as new input. Compounds. Asks: "how does each user generate the next?"

2

Anatomy of a Loop

Every growth loop has the same skeleton: an input, an action the user takes, an output that action produces, and a step where that output is reinvested as the next input.

A content loop, step by step

Input: a new user arrives. Action: they create a public playlist. Output: that playlist gets shared and indexed by search engines. Reinvest: new listeners discover it, arrive, and some create playlists of their own — which is the input again.

The output (new users) circles back to produce more of the input. That closure is what separates a loop from a one-way funnel.

Quick check

In "user creates content → content gets found in search → new users arrive → some create content," what makes this a loop rather than a funnel?

3

Three Common Loop Types

Most growth loops fall into three families, based on what the reinvested output is: other users, content, or money.

Viral loop

Users bring in other users directly — invites, referrals, or collaboration that exposes non-users to the product.

Content loop

Users (or the product) generate content that attracts new users via search or sharing, some of whom generate more content.

Paid loop

Revenue from users funds acquisition of more users, whose revenue funds more acquisition — only sustainable while a customer earns back more than they cost.

Quick check

A company reinvests subscription revenue into paid ads that acquire more subscribers, whose subscription revenue funds still more ads. Which loop type is this?

4

Why Loops Compound (or Stall)

A loop's health comes down to three things: how fast each turn completes (cycle time), how many new inputs each turn produces (the loop factor), and the friction at each step. Small changes here compound dramatically — in both directions.

Cycle time

How long one full turn of the loop takes. Shorter cycles compound faster.

Loop factor

New inputs produced per cycle. Above 1 and the loop grows; below 1 and it shrinks.

Friction

Drop-off at any step chokes the whole loop; the weakest step caps it.

Loops run in reverse too: if the loop factor drops below 1, decline feeds decline.

Quick check

A viral loop where, over time, each user brings in an average of 0.8 new users. What happens to the loop?
